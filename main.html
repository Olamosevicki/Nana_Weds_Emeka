#love at first worship


how we met

I had just come out of a bad relationship and was done.I had
told my sisters "For the next one year, I just want to focus
on my self and my relationship with God.
No guy should come and tell me anything, or ask for my number,
they shouldn't even tell me hi, everyone should just face their
front please".
Then on the last day of Yashab 2.1( @ The Grace Trybe) this
handsome dark skinned guy did a ministration.The moment he
opened his mouth to sing, I was hooked.In my mind I said
" God see this hot chocolate, I want " it was just a silly
thought because I had never seen or noticed him before. I
didn't even know we attended the same branch and I wasn't
expecting to see him after that day.
At the end of the program a friend introduced my
sisters and me to him, we said hi, and the next moment,he was
off, and that was that.
Imagine my shock when I walked into church some Saturdays
later ( for workers prep service ) and noticed him looking
right at me. There were at least 50 people there, so I just
brushed it off that he must have been looking at someone else.
But this went on for weeks, I'll feel someone starring at me,
and he'll be the one. So one day, I decided to put it to test.
When I noticed him staring, I smiled AND HE SMILED BACK!!
Lol I almost had an heart attack that day. After the service,
he came over and said hi and that was that.
Coincidentally, he was in the Tag team department but before
I joined he switched to the choir, but he was still on the Tag
team's WhatsApp group. That's how this man droped a comment on
the group chat that I should DM him, we had alot to do together.
I, thinking he was talking about business, did.That conversation
lasted for days. We talked and talked for hours. We had alot in
common but at the same time had alot to learn from each other.
We spent all the time glued to our phones in steady conversation.
At the end of two months, it was clear that I was intensely
attracted to him. But he wasn't giving any signals so I tried
to clear that thought from my head. ( Tried and failed )
After some time, he told me he wanted a serious relationship
with me. When I read that message, I dropped my phone and cried
a little. Like what are the odds? I remembered the silly little
prayer at Yashab and I just felt so loved by God.
God reminded me that he is interested in even the littlest of
things that concern us. After some minutes, I cleaned my eyes,
picked up my phone and proceeded to do my "Shakara" lol. How
else is a girl supposed to know that a guy was serious? Plus
I needed time to be sure I had healed and moved on from my
previous relationship.
From the moment I decided to DM him, I found a best friend,
a lover, a partner, a supporter, a prayer partner. I have
never had a reason to doubt or second guess my decision.
I am so excited and so grateful to God that I get to
spend the rest of my life with my best friend. My Hot Chocolate.
- Naomi



It's indeed love at first worship because I'd never forget that
day at YASHAB when I first saw these three sisters and knew that
one of them was going to be mine.
I'll never forget one Saturday before choir rehearsals started
I was in the church auditorium, and I prayed and told God the
specifications I wanted in a lady. God sent Naomi who had all
that, and even more.
I'll never forget those beautiful nights where we'll talk on
the phone for hours on end and could never have enough of each other.
I had to dig out my baby's number from Tag Team's group chat. I did
my observation, calculated all the calculatables and well,
shot my shot and the rest they say, is history.
Imagine this girl keeping me hanging for six good months and
then eventually agreeing to date me on my birthday in 2021.
Amazingly I waited. I have never waited that long for any lady
in my life. This is how I knew that I really love Naomi my homie.
Ours is a story of love, patience, trust, learning, unlearning,
faith, and a testament to God's faithfulness.
I bless the day I met you Naomi, and I know that our story will
be one of the best love stories ever told.
- Emeka
